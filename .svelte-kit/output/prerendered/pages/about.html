We design for change

As a strategy and design studio we help movements and organisations shift narratives and move hearts. We have a track record of creating campaigns with real impacts for positive change.

We can help you design everything you need to win. From a website or social media campaign, to printed materials or a mobilisation strategy.

Applying a user-centred design process we can facilitate your team through the process of defining who you need to reach and how design can help you communicate to and organise with them. Workshopping ideas with you to find a strategy that suits your project.

Choosing our studio will subsidise social change.

We run on a “pay what you should” basis. Working for organisations that can afford to cover our full costs allows us to give a third of our time to grassroots campaigns for reduced rates or pro bono. We’re always happy to work with clients to help find a scope and budget we can both afford.

Who we are

Moa Pårup is an activist, designer and art director, who believes in the power of direct action, print design and memes.

With more than ten years experience working with clients within the cultural sector on identities, books, magazines and exhibitions. Moa brings her professional experience to designing campaigns and creating content for social movements. She coordinated the XR UK Design Group between 2020 and 2023 and has a Master’s Degree in Visual Communication from the Royal College of Art.

Liam Geary Baulch is an artist, activist, copywriter and campaign strategist who focuses on creating change through art and activism.

With over twelve years of creative campaigning experience from healthcare and housing to combating the arms trade and the climate crisis. Liam has combined his activist and artistic praxis to achieve campaign wins like Save Lewisham Hospital and global attention grabbing civil disobedience. As part of the team that launched Extinction Rebellion in 2018, Liam designed strategies for mass protest and impactful messaging from press stunts to social content.

Erik Hartin is a climate activist, designer, editor and writer who designs sustainable and regenerative systems to help drive cultural change.

With more than fifteen years of experience working with editorial design and content for cultural institutions, social movements, artists and change makers. With a background in contemporary art and publishing, Erik’s work operates in liminal spaces: between offline mass participation and online culture; between design history and web3 and between collapse and promise. He was selected for the 2023 PhD cohort as Critical Decade for Climate Change Leverhulme Doctoral Scholars at ClimateUEA.
